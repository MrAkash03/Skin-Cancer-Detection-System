  • About

    This project aims to predict skin diseases using advanced machine learning models. By employing a combination of YOLOv8 and ResNet, we classify skin images into benign and malignant categories. The approach uses ensemble learning techniques, where the predictions from both models are combined through majority voting for better accuracy. The workflow consists of image preprocessing, model predictions, and post-processing, along with accuracy metrics to assess performance. Ultimately, this project delivers precise skin disease classifications and ensures reliable outcomes through real-time deployment-ready models.

  • Treatment

    1. Malignant Melanoma Treatment

    Malignant melanoma is a serious form of skin cancer, requiring intensive treatments like surgery, immunotherapy, and targeted therapies. Common options include:

    • Surgery: Excise the cancerous area along with surrounding tissue.
    • Immunotherapy: Checkpoint inhibitors to boost the body's immune response to cancer cells.
    • Radiation Therapy: Used in more advanced cases.

    2. Benign Melanocytic Lesions

    Non-cancerous lesions often don't require major treatment but may be removed for cosmetic reasons or if any irregular changes are detected.

    • Excision: Removal of the benign lesion if necessary.
    • Cryotherapy: Freezing of the lesion for removal.
